The Problem We Are Solving

Educational Gap

South Africa's school curriculum fails to adequately teach students about current politics, economic systems, or financial literacy. Most young people graduate without understanding how their country works.

Information Desert

Mainstream news is written for adults with existing political and economic knowledge. Young people, especially from township communities, find it inaccessible and irrelevant to their lives.

Short Attention Span

The generation raised on TikTok and Instagram requires information in shorter, more visually engaging formats. Traditional articles and TV news lose this audience entirely.

Financial Illiteracy

59.7% youth unemployment, yet most young South Africans have never been taught about savings, investment, property ownership, or how to build wealth in SA's economic system.

The Story Behind YouthPolitics

My name is Bongiwe Mokhati, and I am a Computer Science student at Tshwane University of Technology. YouthPolitics is an idea that I came up with and developed independently based on a challenge that I personally experienced.

As a young person, I wanted to understand what was happening in politics, the economy, and current affairs. However, I often found it difficult to access information that was simple, engaging, and easy to understand. Most news platforms seemed to be designed for people who already had a strong background in politics or economics, while many young people like myself were left feeling confused or overwhelmed.

The inspiration for YouthPolitics came from my own desire to learn. I wanted a platform that could educate me about political events, economic issues, and how these topics affect my daily life. While researching and spending time on social media platforms such as TikTok, I realized that many other young people faced the same problem. The issue was not that they did not care about politics or the economy; rather, they did not know where to start, and the available information was often too complex or inaccessible.

This led me to create YouthPolitics as a solution. The platform is designed to make learning about politics, economics, and current affairs easier, more engaging, and more accessible for young people. It provides information in a way that is understandable, relevant, and connected to real-life experiences. Through YouthPolitics, users can learn about political developments, economic trends, government decisions, elections, and other important topics that shape South Africa and the world.

YouthPolitics is more than just a website; it is a project that reflects my passion for learning and my commitment to helping other young people become informed citizens. What started as a tool to make my own life easier and help me understand the world around me has evolved into a platform that can educate, empower, and inspire other young people to become more knowledgeable and actively involved in society.

I proudly claim YouthPolitics as my original idea, developed from my personal experiences, curiosity, and desire to create a solution that addresses a real challenge faced by many young people.

My vision: To build a generation of informed youth who have the knowledge and confidence to engage in discussions, make informed decisions, and contribute meaningfully to the future of South Africa.

Our Solution — What YouthPolitics Does

YouthPolitics is a free, mobile-first web platform that delivers political education, economic literacy, historical context, and financial skills — in plain, accessible language — built specifically for South Africa's black youth.

🏛️ SA Politics

Complete political party profiles (ANC, EFF, DA, MK Party), the full history of black politics from 1912 to 2024, political family trees explaining key figures, the history of state capture, the 2024 election results analysis, and how the GNU works.

🌍 World Politics

How global events (Russia-Ukraine war, Israel-Gaza, US elections, climate change) directly affect South African prices, jobs, and daily life. SA's role in BRICS, the AU, G20, and the WTO explained clearly.

💰 Economy & Financial Literacy

Inflation, GDP, interest rates, and unemployment explained in plain language with real examples. Step-by-step guides to saving, budgeting, ETF investing, property investment, NSFAS, and building wealth from zero.

📰 News Explained

Complex news stories broken down with context. Historical background provided so readers understand WHY things are happening. Fake news guidance and trusted sources list.

🤖 AI Assistant

Built-in AI chatbot that can answer any question about SA politics, economy, history, or finance in plain language — like a knowledgeable friend with no political agenda.

⚡ TikTok-style Learning Cards

Bite-sized, swipeable learning cards designed for short attention spans — each explaining one concept in under 60 seconds, with a "go deeper" button for those who want more.

Students & Researchers We Are Looking For

To expand this platform's depth and accuracy, we are seeking collaboration with students specializing in the following fields. These students would contribute research, fact-checking, content writing, or technical development.

1. Political Science & International Relations

Why: To deepen coverage of SA political history, party ideology analysis, and how international organizations (BRICS, AU, UN) affect SA youth.

Contribution: Research papers on political movements, historical analysis, party policy breakdowns, explanations of world events.

Political parties SA History Decolonization Black Consciousness Pan-Africanism

2. Economics & Development Studies

Why: To provide accurate, current economic analysis — explaining how GDP, unemployment, fiscal policy, monetary policy, and global trade affect ordinary South Africans.

Contribution: Translating economic data into accessible explanations, analyzing government budgets, explaining the impact of SARB decisions.

Macroeconomics Development economics Fiscal policy Budget analysis Youth unemployment

3. Journalism & Media Studies

Why: To create clear, engaging, bias-free content that breaks down complex news stories in ways that a 16-year-old in Soweto can understand.

Contribution: News articles with context, explainer content, interviewing political figures and activists, media literacy resources.

Investigative journalism Community media Explainer content Digital storytelling

4. History (especially South African & African History)

Why: Many news events can only be understood with historical context. We need deep research on black political history, the liberation movement, apartheid's economic legacy, and post-1994 developments.

Contribution: Historical timelines, biographical profiles of political figures, analysis of how history shapes current politics.

Anti-apartheid history Pan-African history Liberation movements Post-apartheid SA

5. Financial Management & Personal Finance

Why: To create practical, SA-specific guides on budgeting, saving, investing, property, tax, and building wealth that are accurate and actionable for youth with low starting incomes.

Contribution: Financial literacy modules, investment guides, case studies showing how young SA people built wealth, analysis of financial products available to youth.

Personal finance Investing Property TFSA / ETFs Entrepreneurship

6. Computer Science / Web Development

Why: To improve the platform's technical capabilities — better UI/UX, offline functionality (for users with limited data), improved AI integration, and potentially a mobile app.

Contribution: Technical development, optimization for low-bandwidth users, AI chatbot improvement, progressive web app features, data visualization.

Web development AI/ML Mobile development UX design Accessibility

7. Education & Curriculum Development

Why: To ensure content is pedagogically sound and appropriate for different age groups and literacy levels. To potentially align content with the SA school curriculum.

Contribution: Learning pathway design, age-appropriate language guidelines, alignment with CAPS curriculum, assessment tools.

Curriculum design Pedagogy Educational technology CAPS alignment

8. Sociology & Social Justice

Why: To ensure our content accurately reflects the lived experiences of black youth in townships, understands intersectionality, and doesn't inadvertently reproduce inequality.

Contribution: Community feedback research, analysis of how policies affect different communities, social justice framing, youth surveys.

Youth sociology Social justice Community research Intersectionality

Our Vision

A generation of young black South Africans who are politically informed, economically literate, and empowered to actively shape their country's future.

We believe that knowledge is the foundation of power. When young people understand how the economy works, they can navigate it. When they understand political history, they can demand accountability. When they understand the world, they can position themselves to benefit from its opportunities.

Knowledge is Power — this is our message. This platform is a tool to empower the next generation of informed, engaged South African citizens.
