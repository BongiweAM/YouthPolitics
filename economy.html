Economy Explained

Understanding the Economy — No Jargon

What is Inflation?

Know This
5.3%Current SA Rate (June 2026)
7.8%Food Inflation

Real-World Impact:

  • If bread cost R20 last year → it costs R21.06 now
  • Transport costs up 6.9% in one year
  • Your R100 in savings loses R5.30 of value per year
  • Salary increases below 5.3% = you're getting poorer

What to Do

Keep as little cash as possible idle. Put money in savings accounts, investments or assets that grow faster than inflation. Target investments returning 7%+ per year.

Interest Rates Explained

Know This
8.00%Prime Rate (June 2026)
SARBSets the repo rate

How It Works:

  • SARB raises rates → borrowing gets more expensive → people spend less → inflation slows
  • SARB cuts rates → borrowing gets cheaper → more spending → economy grows
  • High rates hurt home loan affordability
  • High rates are GOOD for people with savings accounts

What to Do

When rates are high: pay off debt fast, delay taking big loans. When rates fall: good time to buy property. Track SARB announcements (they happen every 2 months).

What is GDP?

Understand This
1.2%SA Q1 2026 Growth
R8.1TSA's GDP (2026)

Why It Matters:

  • GDP = total value of everything SA produces in a year
  • 1.2% growth is better but not enough to reduce unemployment
  • SA needs 4%+ growth to create enough jobs for 800K new workers per year
  • Load shedding improvements helping economy grow faster

What to Do

Understand which sectors are growing (tech, green energy, services) and align your career or business with those sectors. Don't fight the economic current — swim with it.

Financial Literacy

Build Your Financial Future 💰

Schools don't teach you this. But this knowledge is the difference between struggling and thriving.

01

Start a Budget

The 50/30/20 rule: 50% on needs (rent, food, transport), 30% on wants, 20% on savings and investments. Even with a small income, this framework transforms your financial life.

02

Emergency Fund First

Before investing, save 3 months of expenses in a money market account. This protects you when unexpected things happen — no need to borrow at high interest rates.

03

Invest in ETFs

An ETF (Exchange Traded Fund) lets you own a small piece of many companies at once. The Satrix 40 ETF on EasyEquities tracks SA's top 40 companies. Start with R1. Your money grows as SA grows.

04

Tax-Free Savings

Open a Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA). You can invest up to R36,000 per year (R500,000 lifetime) with ZERO tax on returns. This is one of the best financial tools available to any South African.

05

Property Investment

You can get a 100% home loan (no deposit) with good credit. Rental income covers the bond. Property value grows with inflation. Your tenant builds your wealth. This is how generational wealth is created.

06

Build a Side Income

Coding, design, writing, tutoring, driving, photography — any skill becomes money. R2,000/month from a side hustle, invested in ETFs for 20 years at 10% return, becomes R1.5 million.

Policy Impact

Economic Policies That Affect You

YES Program

Available Now
R21bnGovernment investment
1MJobs target over 3 years

How YES Works:

  • Companies get tax incentives to hire youth aged 18–35
  • 12-month paid employment with skills training
  • Focus on digital economy and green jobs
  • Available in all provinces — apply through your local Labour Centre

How to Access It

Register on yes4youth.co.za or visit your nearest Department of Labour office. This is one of the best youth employment opportunities in SA right now.

Green Economy Jobs

Future is Green
200K+Green jobs by 2026
$8.5bnJust Energy Transition fund

Green Careers to Consider:

  • Solar PV installer — high demand, R15K–R40K/month
  • Wind turbine technician — specialized, well-paid
  • Green hydrogen engineer — brand new field
  • Environmental consultant and compliance officer

How to Prepare

Study through TVET colleges for renewable energy courses. They are free or subsidized. The green economy is where the money will be in the next 20 years — position yourself now.
