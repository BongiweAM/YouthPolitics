Corruption & State Capture

Critical

What Happened:

  • Under Zuma (2009–2018), the Gupta family captured state entities

Black Political History

The History of Black Politics in South Africa ✊

You can't understand where SA is today without understanding where it came from. This is your history.

1912

ANC Founded

The African National Congress is founded on January 8, 1912 — making it one of the oldest liberation movements in Africa. Initially non-violent, it sought to unite black South Africans against colonial oppression.

1955

Freedom Charter

The Congress of the People adopts the Freedom Charter at Kliptown, Soweto. "South Africa belongs to all who live in it." It becomes the ANC's foundational document — still referenced today.

1960

Sharpeville Massacre & ANC Banned

69 peaceful protesters killed by police at Sharpeville. The ANC and PAC are banned. Nelson Mandela and others form Umkhonto we Sizwe (MK) — the ANC's armed wing — and go underground.

1969

Steve Biko Founds Black Consciousness

Steve Biko founds the South African Students Organisation (SASO) and develops Black Consciousness ideology — the idea that black people must psychologically liberate themselves first.

1976

Soweto Uprising

Students rise up against Bantu Education and Afrikaans-medium instruction. Hector Pieterson (13) is shot dead. The uprising changes the world's view of apartheid and energizes the liberation movement.

1990

Mandela Released, ANC Unbanned

On February 11, 1990, Nelson Mandela walks free after 27 years. The ANC is unbanned. Negotiations begin to end apartheid. The world watches.

1994

First Democratic Election

April 27, 1994. Every South African, regardless of race, votes for the first time. The ANC wins 62.7%. Mandela becomes president. Apartheid officially ends.

2007–18

State Capture Under Zuma

Jacob Zuma's presidency is marred by corruption. The Gupta family gains control of state entities. An estimated R1.5 trillion is looted. Eskom collapses. SA's credit rating is downgraded. The era is called "State Capture."

2024

ANC Below 50% — GNU Formed

The ANC's 30-year dominance ends. Getting only 40.2%, it forms the Government of National Unity with the DA and other parties. A new chapter in SA democracy begins.

How Government Works

How SA's Government Is Structured

National Government

President Cyril Ramaphosa leads a Cabinet of Ministers. Parliament has 400 seats in the National Assembly.

  • President: Cyril Ramaphosa (ANC)
  • Deputy President: Paul Mashatile (ANC)
  • Finance Minister: Enoch Godongwana
  • Basic Education: Siviwe Gwarube (DA)
  • Higher Education: Blade Nzimande (SACP)

Provincial Government

9 provinces, each with a Premier and provincial legislature. They handle education, healthcare, and local economic development.

  • Gauteng: ANC (largest economy)
  • Western Cape: DA (best-run province)
  • KwaZulu-Natal: ANC/MK coalition tensions
  • Eastern Cape: ANC (high unemployment)
  • Limpopo, Mpumalanga, NW: ANC

How to Vote & Register

  1. Be 18+ and a South African citizen
  2. Register at any IEC station with your ID
  3. Check status at elections.org.za
  4. Vote on election day at your voting station
  5. Each voter gets 2 ballots — national and provincial
